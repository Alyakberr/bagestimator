How Many 80lb Bags of Concrete Do I Need for a 4x4 Post Hole?

For a typical 10 inch diameter hole dug 24 inches deep for a 4x4 post, subtract the buried post's volume from the cylinder volume, then add a 10 percent wastage margin. That generally lands between 1 and 2 bags of 80lb premix per hole, though soil conditions can shift the figure slightly.

How Many Bags of Concrete Do I Need for a 10x10 Slab at 4 Inches Thick?

A 10 foot by 10 foot slab poured 4 inches deep requires about 1.23 cubic yards of concrete once the standard 10 percent wastage buffer is included, which equals roughly 56 bags of 80lb premix.

How Much Gravel Do I Need for a Trench Subbase?

Multiply trench length by width by depth in feet to get cubic feet, divide by 27 for cubic yards, add a 10 percent compaction margin, then multiply by 1.4 tons per cubic yard for crushed stone.
